best fit line doc

16 July 2024

Pros:

> You do the best-fit first try
> The best-fit line is ALWAYS accurate
> No wasting time erasing if you get it wrong

Con

> Only slightly slower than anyhow drawing best-fit line (First try only)

the thing

To draw any line, you need at least two points on the line. Theoretically, if you have two points of a perfect best fit line, you can draw a perfect best-fit. This doccument will show you how to get two such points so you can join them together: the y-intercept of the best-fit and the midpoint of all the points

y-int (+gradient)

Classicwiz (New calc)

Go to Menu > 3 (Statistics) > 2 (y=a+bx) > plot your points in the table provided (make sure the freq column just says 1) > OPTN > Regression Calc

You will be given three values: a, b, r (y = a+bx), ignore r

The a is a y-intercept so one point of the best fit line is (0, a)

The b is the expected gradient so take note of this to check your answer later

Natural VPAM (Old calc)

Go to Menu > 2 (Stat) > 2 (A+Bx) > plot your points in the table provided (make sure the freq column just says 1) > AC > Shift > 1 > 5 (Reg)

From here, press:

1 (A): The y-coordinate of your first point, which will be (0, A)

2 (B): The (accurate) gradient of your graph, which you can take note to check your answer later

midpoint of all the points

It's as simple as it sounds. Just find the midpoint of all the points; this will be your second point

Plot and join these two points together to get a perfect best-fit
